<p><p>Microwave transmission equipment is categorized into two primary types: full indoor and full outdoor systems. Full indoor microwave equipment is housed within a building, providing protection from environmental factors, and is typically used for short-range communication. In contrast, full outdoor systems are designed for installation outside, often on towers or rooftops, enabling long-range transmission with greater coverage. Both types facilitate efficient wireless communication, with the choice depending on specific application needs and environmental considerations.</p></p>
