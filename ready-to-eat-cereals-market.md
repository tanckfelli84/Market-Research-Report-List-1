<p><p>The ready-to-eat cereals market can be categorized into two main types: pure cereals and composite cereals. Pure cereals consist of single grain options like cornflakes or rice puffs, offering straightforward flavors and textures. In contrast, composite cereals blend multiple grains, nuts, seeds, and dried fruits, enhancing nutritional value and taste variety. This diversification in product offerings caters to different consumer preferences, with pure cereals appealing to those seeking simplicity, while composite cereals attract those looking for a more nutrient-rich breakfast option.</p></p>
