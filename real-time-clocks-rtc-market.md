<p><p>Real-Time Clocks (RTCs) are essential components in electronic devices, providing accurate timekeeping even when the primary power source is disconnected. The market is divided into two main types: soft clocks and hard clocks. Soft clocks, often software-based, rely on system processes to maintain time and are less precise. In contrast, hard clocks are dedicated hardware components with built-in oscillators offering higher accuracy and reliability for time-sensitive applications, such as embedded systems and consumer electronics.</p></p>
