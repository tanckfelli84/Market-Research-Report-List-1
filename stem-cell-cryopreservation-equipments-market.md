<p><ul><li>Liquid Phase</li><li>Vapor Phase</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Stem cell cryopreservation equipment is categorized into two main types: liquid phase and vapor phase. Liquid phase cryopreservation involves submerging cell samples directly in liquid nitrogen, maintaining a consistent temperature for optimal preservation. In contrast, vapor phase cryopreservation uses the nitrogen vapor above the liquid nitrogen, providing a slightly warmer environment while still preserving cellular integrity. Both methods are crucial for storing stem cells long-term, with specific applications depending on the sensitivity of the cells and storage requirements.</p></p>
