<p><p>Superconducting Fault Current Limiters (SFCLs) are devices that manage fault currents in electrical systems. Market types include Shielded-Core SFCLs, which utilize a superconducting shield to limit current; Saturable-Core SFCLs, which employ magnetic cores that saturate under high current to prevent excessive flow; Hybrid Resistive SFCLs, combining resistive elements with superconductors for enhanced functionality; and Purely Resistive SFCLs, which use resistors to limit fault currents. Each type offers unique advantages for improving power system stability and protection.</p></p>
